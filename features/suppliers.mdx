**Suppliers** (Proveedores) are the businesses or individuals from whom you buy inventory. Every purchase in Bodequita must be linked to a supplier, so keeping your supplier list up to date is essential for accurate purchase records.

## Supplier Fields

Each supplier record contains the following fields:

| Field | Description | Required | Unique |
|---|---|---|---|
| `id` | Auto-generated internal identifier | Auto | — |
| `ruc` | Tax identification number (RUC) | Yes | Yes |
| `nombre` | Supplier business or contact name | Yes | Yes |
| `telefono` | Phone number | No | No |

Both `ruc` and `nombre` have unique constraints — you cannot have two suppliers with the same RUC or the same name.

## Adding a Supplier

Suppliers are managed through the **Gestionar Proveedor** dialog, accessible from the Purchases screen.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the supplier dialog">
    Go to **Compras** from the drawer, then tap the **Gestionar** button in the Proveedor section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter supplier details">
    Fill in the **RUC**, **Nombre**, and optionally **Teléfono** fields in the dialog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Tap **Guardar**. The supplier is added to the database and immediately available in the supplier dropdown.

    If the RUC already exists, the dialog shows *"El proveedor ya existe"* and no duplicate is created.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editing a Supplier

Supplier names can be updated through the `SupplierViewModel.renameSupplier()` operation. In the current UI, to correct a supplier's details you can:

1. Add a new supplier entry with the corrected RUC and name.
2. Use the existing supplier for future purchases going forward.

<Note>
  Full in-place editing of all supplier fields (RUC, phone) via the UI dialog is available in the Purchases screen when adding a new supplier. Renaming an existing supplier by ID is supported at the ViewModel layer.
</Note>

## Deleting a Supplier

The `SupplierViewModel.deleteSupplier()` operation removes a supplier record from the database. After deletion, the supplier no longer appears in the Purchases dropdown.

<Warning>
  Purchase records that reference a deleted supplier retain the original `proveedor_id` in the database. The purchase history is preserved but the supplier name will no longer be resolvable from the ID. Avoid deleting suppliers who have existing purchase records.
</Warning>

## How Suppliers Are Linked to Purchases

When you register a purchase:

- The selected supplier's `id` is stored as `proveedor_id` in the `Purchase` table.
- This link ties every purchase expense to the supplying entity.
- The **Cash Closure** module uses `purchaseDao.todayPurchasesTotal()` to sum today's purchase costs regardless of supplier, giving you total daily expenses.
